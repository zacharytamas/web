Lifetime Income Process

Highlights

  • Rapidly learned Adobe Flex and ActionScript in order to build project as a cross-platform application.
  • With a colleague, designed and implemented an encrypted data structure that could be written to and read from a file, allowing secure saving of financial planners' customers' data.
  • Implemented complex financial formulas using Calculus and techniques such as Newton-Raphson Iteration.

About the Project

The Lifetime Income Process is a financial model developed by a former Software Engineer, now Financial Planner. The model attempts to answer the question "Will I be able to maintain my standard of living throughout my lifetime?" by taking in all the details of a person's finances and plotting their financial situation across the rest of their lives.

LIP was initially developed as a series of Open Office spreadsheets serving as a proof of concept, then improved over the course of three years. When the model was proven, a colleague and I were approached for the task of building a cross-platform desktop application for licensing to other financial advising practices.

Skills Used/Developed on this Project

This application was developed using Adobe Flex and written in ActionScript and MXML. Graphic design was done in Adobe Photoshop.

Mathematical financial analysis was required, including the need to convert many common spreadsheet formula functions into their ActionScript equivalents. Some of these functions required much research and Calculus derivation to find the most efficient algorithms for computation.
