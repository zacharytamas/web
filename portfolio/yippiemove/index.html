YippieMove

The easy email migration tool.

Highlights

  • Worked 100% remotely with team members across the globe, including California, London, Sweden, and Budapest.
  • Worked across the full-stack, from Python/Django server-side to the browser-side technologies. Also became acquainted with essential email protocols.
  • Contributed to designing and implementing YippieMove's first public API, and an open source command-line utility for working with the API.

The Product

While working for a startup named Wireload, I contributed to the development of YippieMove, an email migration tool used by individuals, corporations, Ivy League universities, and even some Internet service providers.

YippieMove facilitates the easy transfer of an entire email account from one provider to another. Common use cases include:

  • Universities contract YippieMove to move students' emails from their university accounts and to their personal account, allowing the university to delete the student email account after graduation.
  • ISPs contract YippieMove to provide a simple way for their customers switching from other ISPs to transfer their emails into their new ISP email account.
  • Individuals use YippieMove to consolidate their email accounts, or move emails from an old account before safely deleting it.

Skills Used/Developed on this Project

At YippieMove, we used Python with the Django framework. Client-side styling code was written in LESS and compiled to CSS. JavaScript was used with frameworks such as jQuery to provide the interactivity featured in YippieMove's customer migration management panels.

On the backend, YippieMove is powered by load-balanced gunicorn instances connected to PostgreSQL databases with some features using MongoDB. Email account authentication was provided by OAuth2. Migration tasks were managed asynchronously using Celery. Migration servers, "movers", were spun up and down dynamically using CloudSigma as a cloud provider. Payments were processed initially by Google Checkout, but part of my work was to transition to Stripe.
